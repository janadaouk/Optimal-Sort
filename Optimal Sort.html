Graph Explanation: When observing the graph, it is observed that when the file changes, the speed changes as well. So, as the amount of data increases, so does the time. Another factor also affecting the average speeds would be the algorithm being used to sort. Varying between BubbleSort, InsertionSort, and SelectionSort, it is abundantly clear that Insertion sort is the most effective here as it is the fastest among the algorithms; In fact, it was the only algorithm able to sort 10,000 random words without the "CPU time exceeded (core dumped)" error. Other than that, we have the type of data being sorted. Obviously we are alphabetically sorting strings, but in some of the files, the words were already sorted, so no swaps were necessary. Naturally, that takes up time, making the average speed in the ordered the fastest to sort. The amount of data being sorted, its original order, and the type of algorithm you use all affects the time it takes.
